Are Food Imports Hindering Barbados' Development?

Before the global financial crisis of 2008, Barbados' economy was growing steadily. Afterwards, not so much. But how much of their GDP have they been spending on food?

Over the past 30 years, despite their GDP increasing, they have been spending roughly the same fraction of their GDP on food. How does this compare with other islands in the Caribbean and around the world?

The Caribbean islands are all varied in how much of their GDP they spend on food imports. Like the smaller islands Barbados spends a larger share on food but this translates to being the second highest spent per person. Is all of this spending avoidable? Can Barbados produce enough food to to meet the demands of its population?

Barbados devotes over a quarter of its land to crops yet this translates to less that 15 hectares. Importing food is a necessary evil for Barbados as it doesn't have to space to grow its own food.
